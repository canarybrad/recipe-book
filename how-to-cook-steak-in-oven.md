# How to Cook Steak in the Oven

## Ingredients
- 2 steaks, about 1 to 1.5 inches thick (filet mignon, rib eye, T-bone, strip, or sirloin)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon avocado oil or other high-heat cooking oil
- 2 tablespoons butter (for serving)
- Fresh thyme sprigs (for serving)

## Instructions
1. Let steaks rest on a plate until they reach room temperature, about 30-45 minutes.
2. Preheat oven broiler on high. Place an oven-safe skillet (cast iron recommended) in the oven while it preheats.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Blot steaks dry with paper towels. Brush evenly on both sides with oil, then sprinkle seasoning mixture on both sides.
4. Carefully remove the hot skillet from the oven and place on the stovetop over high heat. Sear steaks for 2-3 minutes on one side until a crust forms.
5. Flip the steaks, then transfer the skillet to the oven. Broil for 3 minutes.
6. Flip steaks once more and continue broiling for 2-3 minutes, until internal temperature reaches 5 degrees below your desired doneness.
7. Remove pan from oven and transfer steaks to a clean plate. Brush with pan juices, top with butter and thyme. Loosely tent with foil and rest for at least 3 minutes.

## Temperature Guide
- Rare: 120-125 degrees F
- Medium-Rare: 130-135 degrees F
- Medium: 135-145 degrees F
- Medium-Well: 145-155 degrees F
- Well Done: 155+ degrees F
