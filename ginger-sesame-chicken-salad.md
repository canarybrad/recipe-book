# Grilled Ginger Sesame Chicken Chopped Salad

## Ingredients

### For the Marinade/Dressing
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons finely minced ginger
- 3 tablespoons canola oil
- 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
- 1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil
- 1 teaspoon Sriracha
- 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
- 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
- 1/4 cup chopped green onions

### For the Salad
- 2 (9-ounce) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 lb napa cabbage, halved lengthwise and thinly sliced crosswise
- 1 1/2 to 2 cups matchstick carrots
- 2/3 cup slivered or sliced almonds, toasted
- 1/2 cup cilantro leaves, chopped
- 3 green onions, chopped
- 1 teaspoon white sesame seeds, toasted
- 1 teaspoon black sesame seeds

## Instructions
1. Whisk together soy sauce, ginger, canola oil, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, Sriracha, and salt in a mixing bowl.
2. Add chicken breasts to a resealable bag with 3 tablespoons of the marinade mixture, reserving the remainder. Seal and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 1 day.
3. Combine red wine vinegar and 1/4 cup chopped green onions with the reserved marinade mixture to make the dressing. Set aside.
4. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush lightly with oil and cook marinated chicken about 4 minutes per side until cooked through (165 degrees F internal).
5. Transfer chicken to a cutting board, rest 10 minutes, then cut crosswise into 1/4-inch thick strips.
6. In a large bowl, toss cabbage, chicken, carrots, almonds, chopped green onions, and cilantro with enough dressing to coat.
7. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve.
