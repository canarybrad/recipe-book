# Pork Chops with Apple Cider Glaze

## Ingredients
- 6 boneless pork chops
- 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
- 1 teaspoon pepper (or to taste)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 cups apple cider
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

## Instructions
1. Pat chops dry with paper towels. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chops on both sides until browned, reaching an internal temperature of 145 degrees F.
3. Transfer cooked chops to a plate and cover with foil to rest.
4. Add minced garlic to the same skillet and cook about 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in apple cider vinegar and cook for about one minute, scraping the bottom to loosen browned bits.
5. Add apple cider, Dijon mustard, thyme, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a boil and simmer for 25-30 minutes until reduced to about 2/3 cup. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
6. Return chops to the skillet, coating both sides in glaze. Cook for 2 minutes until heated through, then serve.
