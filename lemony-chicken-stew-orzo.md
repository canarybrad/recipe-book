# Lemony Chicken Stew with Orzo

## Ingredients
- 5 tablespoons canola oil, divided
- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided
- 1 teaspoon freshly ground pepper, divided
- 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
- 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
- 2 ribs celery, chopped
- 1 onion, diced
- 1 leek, thinly sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup orzo pasta
- 5 cups chicken stock
- 1 cup water
- 3 tablespoons lemon juice (or more to taste)
- 2 tablespoons fresh tarragon, chopped

## Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in an 8-quart stockpot over medium-high heat. Season flour with salt and pepper, then dredge chicken pieces in the flour mixture.
2. Brown half the chicken until golden, about 2 minutes per side, then transfer to a bowl. Add 2 more tablespoons oil and brown remaining chicken. Set aside with the first batch.
3. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to pot with carrots, celery, and onion. Cook over medium heat for 3-5 minutes until onion softens. Add leek and garlic and cook 2 more minutes. Season with remaining salt and pepper.
4. Return chicken to pot with orzo, chicken stock, water, and half the tarragon. Cover and simmer about 15 minutes until orzo is tender and broth thickens slightly, stirring occasionally.
5. Stir in lemon juice and season with additional salt and pepper as needed. Sprinkle with remaining tarragon before serving.
