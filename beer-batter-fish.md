# Beer Batter Fish

## Ingredients

### Fish
- 700g (1.4 lb) white fish fillets (cod, snapper, haddock, or tilapia), skinless and boneless
- 1/4 cup rice flour (for dusting)
- Salt

### Beer Batter
- 3/4 cup plain/all-purpose flour
- 1/4 cup rice flour
- 1 1/4 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup very cold beer

### For Frying
- 4-5 cups peanut oil (or vegetable, canola, or cottonseed oil)

### Serving
- Tartar sauce
- Lemon wedges

## Instructions
1. Pat fish dry with paper towels. Cut into 7x3 cm batons or larger fillets. For thick fillets, cut horizontally in half.
2. Place 1/4 cup rice flour in a shallow bowl for dusting.
3. Heat 6 cm of oil in a large heavy pot to 190°C (375°F).
4. Salt 3-4 fish pieces and coat in rice flour, shaking off excess. Can rest up to 10 minutes.
5. Make the batter: whisk flour, rice flour, baking powder, and salt together. Add cold beer and whisk until just combined. Do not overmix -- lumps are fine.
6. Dunk each floured fish piece into batter and let excess drip off briefly.
7. Carefully lower fish into hot oil one piece at a time. Fry for 3 minutes total, flipping after about 2 minutes, until deep golden.
8. Remove to paper towels to drain. Repeat with remaining fish.
9. Serve hot with tartar sauce and lemon wedges.
