# Black Bean and Spinach Enchiladas

## Ingredients

### Enchilada Sauce
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1/4 cup tomato paste
- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/4 teaspoon chili powder
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Enchiladas
- 1 (15 oz) can black beans, rinsed and drained
- 1 1/2 cups sweet corn (canned or frozen and thawed)
- 6 oz fresh baby spinach
- 6 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1/3 cup cilantro, chopped
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 cup shredded cheese
- 8 large flour tortillas

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add tomato paste, flour, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, and chili powder. Cook 1 minute while whisking.
2. Gradually whisk in vegetable broth and bring to a light boil. Reduce to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened, about 8 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
4. Saute spinach in a pan over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until lightly wilted.
5. In a large bowl, combine black beans, 3/4 cup cheese, wilted spinach, corn, green onions, cilantro, and cumin.
6. Pour a small amount of sauce into a 9x13 inch baking dish to coat the bottom.
7. Fill tortillas generously with mixture, roll tightly with ends tucked in, and place seam-side down in dish.
8. Pour remaining sauce over enchiladas, coating evenly. Sprinkle remaining 1/4 cup cheese on top.
9. Bake for about 20 minutes. Garnish with additional cilantro and green onions before serving.
