# Torre de Fondue de Queso (Cheese Fondue Tower)

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 1 cup (150 g) unbleached all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon instant yeast
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/3 cup (75 ml) warm water
- 2 tablespoons (30 ml) olive oil

### Cheese and Accompaniments
- 3 small wheels of brie or Camembert cheese, about 12 oz (300 g) each, all the same diameter
- 2 tablespoons (30 ml) olive oil, divided
- 1 lb (450 g) small baby potatoes
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Cold cuts and sour gherkins for serving (optional)

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Add warm water and olive oil, mixing until the dough comes together. Knead for 5 minutes on a floured surface until smooth.
2. Shape dough into a ball and place in an oiled bowl. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm spot for 2 hours or until doubled in volume.
3. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (180 degrees C) with rack in the middle position.
4. Using a sharp knife, remove the rind from the top of one cheese wheel. Remove the rind from the top and bottom of the other two cheese wheels and stack them on top of the first to form a tower.
5. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out to form a 10-inch circle. Center the cheese tower on the dough, fold the dough up and over to cover completely, and seal. Place seam-side down in a 13x9-inch baking dish. Let rest 15 minutes. Brush with 1 tablespoon olive oil.
6. Toss baby potatoes with remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange around the cheese tower in the baking dish.
7. Bake for 45 minutes or until the dough is nicely browned.
8. Using a bread knife, remove the top of the dough to reveal the melted cheese inside. If cheese is not fully melted, return to oven for 5 more minutes.
9. Serve immediately from the baking dish, dipping potatoes, bread, cold cuts, or gherkins into the melted cheese.
